📋 How to Run This Practice Round

  1. Two teams of 5 work separately. Open this page on two laptops/tabs — one per team. Each team has its own private 30-minute round.
  2. Quick self-organization (1 min per team): skim all 10 problems, split into 2 sub-pods by topic strength (e.g., "geometry" and "number theory/algebra"), pick a "scribe" who types each agreed-upon answer into the correct box, and a "checker" who verifies before the scribe types it.
  3. Click ▶ Start. Teams have 30 minutes — this is a stretch round; they're not expected to finish all 10. Problems are ordered easy → hard (Q1 easiest, Q10 hardest). The strongest teams might solve 7–8; a finish of 5–6 is solid. Encourage them to triage: skim all 10 in the first 60 seconds, lock in the easy ones (Q1, Q3, Q9), then tackle the medium block (Q2, Q4, Q5, Q6), and only attempt Q7/Q8/Q10 if time allows.
  4. When the timer hits 0:00: all answer boxes lock automatically. Teams cannot type or change anything after this point. No grading yet — no answers shown.
  5. Teacher unlocks grading: walk to each laptop and type the secret phrase into the unlock box at the top of the page. The system instantly auto-grades all 10 answers, paints ✓ or ✗ next to each, and reveals detailed step-by-step explanations under every wrong (or blank) answer.
  6. Compare scores between teams and walk through the missed problems together as a class. The detailed explanations are written in plain teacher-walkthrough style — read them aloud verbatim.

Coaching tip: Walk between teams during the round. Don't give answers; ask "what does this remind you of?" or "have you tried small cases?" Watch which kids naturally lead, verify, or hang back — that information is more valuable than the score.
