📋 How to Run This Practice Round

  1. Two teams of 5 work separately. Open this page on two laptops/tabs — one per team.
  2. Quick self-organization: skim all 10 problems, split into 2 sub-pods by topic strength (e.g., "geometry" and "number theory/algebra"), pick a "scribe" who types each agreed-upon answer into the correct box, and a "checker" who verifies before the scribe types it.
  3. Click ▶ Start. Teams solve at their own pace. Problems are ordered easy → hard (Q1 easiest, Q10 hardest). Encourage them to triage: skim all 10 first, lock in the easy ones, then tackle the medium block, and only attempt the hardest ones after that.
  4. When ready to review: click Show Answers. The system auto-grades all 10 answers, paints ✓ or ✗ next to each, and reveals detailed step-by-step explanations under every wrong (or blank) answer.
  5. Compare scores between teams and walk through the missed problems together as a class. The detailed explanations are written in plain teacher-walkthrough style — read them aloud verbatim.

Coaching tip: Walk between teams during the round. Don't give answers; ask "what does this remind you of?" or "have you tried small cases?" Watch which kids naturally lead, verify, or hang back — that information is more valuable than the score.
